6/13

Woke up at 8. Took MRT to Beimen (transfer at Guting) and met Chia-Wei there at 9. Took us about 30 minutes to actually find the venue. When we arrived, we got cool packets and lanyards. Met Hung Lo, the delegate whose English is very good. Competed a bit and had to judge two blindfolded solves.During blindfolded, the room was very empty because of lunch, but the people who were still there were extremely quiet. Two kids asked me to help them fix their popped 4x4. Chia Wei and I picked up lunch at McDonalds closeby. In the 2nd round of 3x3, I was very nervous I wouldn't make the finals, something I never feel at US competitions. I didn't even make the 2nd rounds of 4x4 and 5x5 - people here were so fast. I did manage to get 3rd in 2x2 with a nice 1.79 single though, and 3rd in skewb. And first in BLD with a 47.22. Won some very cool medals. Afterwards, Sam, Alvin, Sam's cousin William and I met with another one of Sam's cousins, Benson, and two of his friends - Josh and Jackie. We went to a newly opened mall (it's actually still unfinished!) called Syntrend. It focuses almost solely on technology and was AWESOME. There were 7 floors we could access, as floors 8-12 were still being constructed. Each floor has a different focus. The gaming floor was very cool; there were gaming laptops and desktops setup for anyone to play on (League, DotA, CS:GO, etc). For dinner we ate noodle cakes(?) with sauce and meat. Very delicious. Also had more boba. Other floors had technology stores with mechanical keyboards, laptops with projectors built-in, the latest phones, driving simulators, etc. My favorite was the headphones. Listening to some (specifically the Beats, surprisingly) were surreal. Eventually took MRT home and played some poker.
